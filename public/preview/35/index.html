Profile
Wisconsin Rapids, Wisconsin

John Moss

Entrepreneur, ag-tech visionary, and fourth-generation cranberry farmer building systems that bridge innovation and tradition.

In the heart of Wisconsin's cranberry country, where morning mist rises from ancient bogs and the landscape stretches to meet the horizon, John Moss is quietly building the future of agriculture. At 40, he straddles two worlds — the deeply rooted traditions of a family farm that spans four generations, and the cutting edge of agricultural technology.

It's a balance that defines not just his work, but his philosophy. Every venture, every system, every decision flows from a single conviction: build things that last.

"To build things that last — foundations that turn chaos into clarity through systems that reduce friction, create leverage, and leave people more capable than when I found them."

John Moss

The 150-acre Elm Lake Cranberry farm has been in the Moss family for over a century. But under John's stewardship, it has become something more — a living laboratory where drones survey the bogs, GIS systems map every square foot, and autonomous equipment works alongside human hands that have known this land for generations.

Where Heritage Meets Innovation

From this foundation, John has built Elm Lake Labs — an agricultural technology company delivering GIS services, drone operations, and precision farming solutions. He founded Blossom, a SaaS platform designed to streamline the complex operations that modern farmers face daily. He serves as an FJ Dynamics dealer, bringing autonomous agricultural robotics to the heartland.

His leadership extends beyond his own ventures. As President of the Wisconsin Cranberry Board, he guides the industry. In 2026, he joins the Board of Directors at Ocean Spray, bringing his unique perspective as both a grower and a technologist to one of agriculture's most iconic cooperatives.
