Philosophy

How I Think

"To build things that last — foundations that turn chaos into clarity through systems that reduce friction, create leverage, and leave people more capable than when I found them."

I'm a fourth-generation steward of a century-old family operation in central Wisconsin. Known industry-wide as the "tech-forward" grower, I've spent the last decade bridging the gap between agricultural tradition and modern technology.

My approach is rooted in systems thinking. Every project I take on — whether it's running a 150-acre cranberry farm, building software for growers, or serving on industry boards — shares the same foundation: reduce friction, create leverage, compound over time.

The cranberry industry doesn't need another gadget. It needs infrastructure — the kind of foundational work that makes everything downstream easier. That's what I build.
