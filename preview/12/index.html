The Story

Standing at the edge of a cranberry marsh in central Wisconsin, it's easy to see only what's on the surface — 150 acres of rich, dark bogs stretching toward the tree line, much as they have for over a century. But beneath the still waters and vibrant fruit, a quiet revolution is underway.

John Moss, 40, is the fourth generation of his family to steward Elm Lake Cranberry. But unlike his predecessors, who relied on intuition and tradition, Moss has woven technology into every layer of the operation — from precision irrigation systems that respond to real-time environmental data to drone-mapped field surveys that reveal what the eye cannot see.

"Most people think farming and technology are separate worlds," Moss says. "But every generation of my family has adopted the best tools available to them. We're just doing the same thing — the tools have changed."

That pragmatic philosophy has made Moss something of an anomaly in the cranberry industry — a grower who speaks as fluently about GIS platforms and SaaS products as he does about soil pH and frost management. Through Elm Lake Labs, his agricultural technology venture, he's now extending those tools beyond his own operation.
